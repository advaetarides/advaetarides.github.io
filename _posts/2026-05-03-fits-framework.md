# F.I.T.S.

> Don't just pick a bike — find your FITS.

The [Three T's](/blog/2026-05-03-three-ts-framework.html) told you if your **body** fits the bike. FITS tells you if your **life** fits the bike.

Answer four questions. Get your motorcycle category.

---

## The Four Axes

| | Factor | Question |
|---|---|---|
| **F** | Frequency | How often will you ride? |
| **I** | Intensity | How fast do you want to go? |
| **T** | Terrain | Where are you riding? |
| **S** | Span | How long is each ride? |

Each axis runs from 1 to 5. Your combination points to a motorcycle category.

---

## F — Frequency

**Rare (1)** → You ride weekends when the sun is out. Maintenance isn't a concern.

**Daily (5)** → This is your commuter. It needs to start every morning, rain or shine. Reliability and low maintenance are everything.

*High F bikes: Adventure, Touring, Dual Sport, Scooter*

---

## I — Intensity

**Relaxed (1)** → You ride for the feeling. Speed doesn't matter. The journey is the destination.

**Aggressive (5)** → You want acceleration. Lean angles. The track. Every ride is an event.

*High I bikes: Supersport, Hyper Naked*

---

## T — Terrain

**City (1)** → Smooth tarmac, traffic lights, filtering between cars.

**Off-Road (5)** → Gravel, dirt, potholes, mountain passes. The road quality is unpredictable.

*High T bikes: Adventure, Dual Sport*

---

## S — Span

**15 minutes (1)** → Quick commute. Comfort doesn't matter.

**6+ hours (5)** → All-day rides. Your back, wrists, and backside need to survive the distance.

*High S bikes: Adventure, Touring, Sport Tourer*
